I am Sam Parks. A pale and skinny magenta-colored axolotl born in one of the many earths. This is how I was born, this is how I lived with my life. It all came back to me.

*2752 (2006 - 20A) - 2/14*

"...Now, you can uncover your eyes." My older brother, Surl, spoke to me as he led me somewhere special.

I opened my eyes, and there I saw it, space. Just stellar constellations, nebulas, planets, it felt so surreal. I felt strange about how space travel is possible, yet it's so gatekept. People on my earth, many of my classmates had no idea, but I was an exception. My brother Surl worked as one of the many captains for the Dawn of the Green Light. What I always thought was an armed corporation, back then I never gave it much thought.

"Sam... Can't wait for you to grow up lil' bro!" He gave me a warm, energetic smile, "I desperately need someone to cover my back, especially when I'm out there doing... Stuff. It gets real boring without anyone familiar out there, I'm always paired with just random people."

"What about Sue?" I asked, still somewhat lost in the sight.

"I'll see about that." He thought for a moment, "It was tough getting you here in the first place! You know our parents aren't really into my antics. I don't know how it'll be with Sue."

There was some chat, a lot that I don't remember much of.

"...Remember, you can't tell anyone about this back home."

My first day of high school.

"...Okay everyone, I'll be your homeroom teacher for the semester..." I forgot her name, "...Come up to introduce yourselves."

Many of the classmates were kids I knew since I was born. Most of us lived in the same small town, though there were some new faces.

"Call me Ena."

*2008 - 20A 8/12*

I invited Ferd to hangout and have a sleepover at my place because it's been a long while. We played a lot of Craftermine on the Zebox 720, that split-screen mode was fun. I didn't play that game much, but Ferd was obsessed over it. Our friend-group had one base for years on the game, and he kept adding onto it and improving our builds, he was so talented, he was... I remember it struck me at that moment. His warm smile, his infectious laughter when he broke out of his shyness. We held hands as kids, we...

"Are you okay?" He asked me with concern while I was in deep thought, I snapped back to reality.

"Y-yeah, yeah." I nodded, turning back to focus on the game.

I kept looking at his hands, and him while he was focused on the game, why did *love* hit me there? I couldn't sleep that night while he was snoozing his brains out. I couldn't stop blushing. The following day felt really weird, especially after he left without a single clue.

*2008 - 20A 9/4*

The next high school semester started. I was confident this would be my year, I'd ask him out sometime, it would be normal for a dude to ask another dude out, right?! Not like he would judge me, right?! During class we were assigned group projects for the month. Two people per project. I landed with Beston, he landed with... Ena. 

Ena was a complete menace, no way he would be willing to stay with her. Right after class I shot out to find Ferd after chatting with Beston about the project. I looked at the class where he went with Ena for some reason and they were nowhere?!

*2008 - 20A 9/21*

Ferd was gone for three weeks, and this day him and a few other classmates were finally found. We weren't told where, or how, but they were in the hospital and they let us in. I was at the back, but once I finally got to say something to Ferd, I chickened out.

Their recovery was surprisingly fast, turns out nothing life-threatening happened to them.

Footsteps come closer in the quiet night.

"...Here, the defeated hero lies." A familiar voice slides by Sam's ear, poking at him with a long wooden staff.

"What do you want." Sam, in their lowest laid in the sand far away from all contact, intentionally waiting to be eaten by nature.

"What was your name again? Or what I heard my friend say, Ally." Yzahela thought for a moment. "You're one interesting character."

"I'm a fraud, I betrayed everyone, leave me be." He turned to face away from her.

"Have you?" Suddenly, she appeared in front of his face, "Nothing happened."

"What?" He was confused.

"You still have to choose." She went on, "Whether you go back to your roots, Sam, or whether you become a local, Ally."

"How do you know my name..?" He was even more confused.

"I'm old! I know a lot of things." She chuckled, "I came here to visit my friend, but you truly interest me." She offered her blackened hand to Sam, "Call me Hazy. Normally this is a secret between me and the others, but you sure impressed Emmy!"

Nervously, he accepted the handshake, "What are you?"

"I'm an Inka." She revealed herself, "Divine beings which are neither angels or demons. We were born to fix mistakes, the little problems within strings that could force the worlds to lose their color. The little strings that cannot be fixed by the grander beings. That's what we were destined to do, but it has lost most of it's meaning as time marches on. I just wander around, really."

"Okay..." He nodded, without another word to say.

"I want to bring you somewhere, are you interested?" She politely asked.

In nervous silence, they nodded again and took her hand. They walked at the edges of the sand dunes under the large and bright full moon with her camel following behind. At the far distance on the horizon, three giant walkers were seen marching on to a distant light that polluted the sky. Guilt fell over their mind, they wanted to say something, but cut themself off and kept silent. Hazy knew they had something to say, she was quietly smiling.

"There once were great rivers in this land, the towns were built here, now it's nothing but dunes." She softly talked, "That's the cruel reality, something's just come without anyway to stop them from happening."

They both suddenly stopped before a pit obscured by dried bushes and surprisingly fresh palm trees, "Ah, this place, you may need it dear."

Hazy let them step through first, and what was covered by nature was something they could not believe. Water, a whole clear oasis. They shook at the mere sight, and immediately ran for it to get a grasp of the water.

"What was it that you once wrote in a journal..." Hazy thought to herself, "Something may come true, I feel it."

They drank as if their life depended on it.

"I would be careful." She smiled.

He stopped, looked back at her, but the reflection wasn't following his moves. From the surface of the water, from his reflection, Ally grabbed onto Sam's arms and dragged them deep into the water. He felt like drowning, but after a moment, there was nothing but emptiness and just them both.

"Sam." She said, pointing at him.

"Ally." He said, pointing at her.

Ally was paler than Sam, a white axolotl with somewhat more feminine features. For the first time Sam didn't despise his reflection, but deep down he knew this is only what he always envisioned himself as. Ally spread her arms out for a hug, her form was only blurry. Sam stepped closer, nervous, scared, but he accepted her hug, and it felt like both their souls shined with love. 

"Will you be okay with me?" Sam got slightly emotional, he still knew who he was.

"Of course." She smiled, "You're me, after all."

Hazy climbed the dune to look at the horizon where the three walkers marched, the first rays of the suns began to envelop the world.

"Make the right choice, Ally."
