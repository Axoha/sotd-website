"...Here, the slumbering hero lies. Unintentionally waiting to be eaten by vultures amidst the scorching drylands where the floor splits for its breath. As our hero finally wakes, with their ears ringing against the blinding sun, they-"

"...Where- Where am I."

"Aw, you were supposed to wait for your queue." An unknown figure, dressed in layers of thin clothes adored with carefully-vowed patterns stood from their small resting spot. "No fun in that now." The bottles hanging from their bag made distracting clanking noises, the hot wind only blew slightly.

"Who are you?!" The vultures which have circled above him dispersed, "Where- What happened?!"

The traveler walked up to him, offering shade with their clothes and silhouette, "How many layers of your skin have you shed so far in this heat?"

He made confused, tired expressions, "Sorry, what?"

The travelled pulled out a small, hand mirror from their bag, "Do you recognize this person, dear?"

"Um." He looked at himself. Burnt, bruised, ugly, "Yeah, that's... no."

"You were asking so many questions, I was sure you lost your mind even going out into these wastelands alone. Axolotl's don't last long out in the sun. Thought you'd have some water, foreign strangers usually got loads, 'specially those greens."

He groaned, "Where am I."

"Hmph." The traveler looked at him, "Hold my hand, I know a place." They said with a smile beneath the hood.

"...Sure." He replied. With a weakened arm and grip he hung onto the stranger's hand, getting up with the help of a slight push. Before he knew it, they were both saddled on a bizarre looking camel, adored with old phones and other electronic cables for decoration around its neck.

"Ain't from 'round here, are you? You came in at one of the worse times dear, it'll be a miracle you last here more than a week." They stirred up a chat, "Name's Yzahela, you?"

He was following along until she asked that question. At that moment, he reached out into the deepest parts of his mind. Static, fog, nothing. No recollection of anything they ever done, no memory, just the hanging thread of skills, words, and survival instinct. He zoned out in front of her, staring blankly into the town ahead of them.

"You good?" Yzahela waved at his face, "Don't tell me you forgot your name, everyone's got a name! People find a random thing and suddenly that's their name, it's easy!" She cackled. "Even those abandoned, dear."

"Oh, sorry." He awkwardly smiled, "Where are you taking me..?"

"Johnny." She looked at the place just beyond the horizon, "One of the seven only towns on this continent, built from walkers forgotten by time. You can see its head poking out! Waving, shaking through the heat, oh... How I miss the days of rain."

"Rain?" Confused, he asked.

Yzahela looked at him.

"You must be from 'round here, after all." She turned back at the direction they were headed. "Taken away, robbed, knocked out to be left for dead, oh that's how the cruel mother of this land is. I saw familiar traces of steps in the dirt, it all makes sense now."

He only stared at her and nodded, he didn't have much to add into the conversation, anyway, just more questions and questions. Once the two suns were right above them,

"Here it is." She got off the camel, "Town of Johnny."

The air lingered of a putrid smell and dust from the land around. The trees were dead, buildings were made of dried logs and sunburnt copper plates. Doohickeys were placed around the sign which held the name of this town, making ticking and clacking noises when the hot wind blew. No one was outside, not during the hottest time of the day.

"I have to go now, dear." She smiled, "Sell my wares, I'll soon move on from this town to another. It was nice seeing you, traveler."

And once she jumped back on the camel, "Oh! I almost forgot..." She rummaged through her bag, only to take out a dead phone and a plastic bottle with dirty water. With a soft fling, he managed to catch the things in the air with both of his hands. "Get yourself something good, last for more than a day, will ya?"

"Okay..." He gave an awkward smile as she departed on her strange camel, not waving back, not understanding what it meant.

He stood at the middle of the town's square, the surroundings were strangely hilly.

Suddenly, a voice echoed in his head.

"*...Snacks.*"

The wind blew, pointing a weather vane with a screeching noise of metal directly onto a unique building.

"I'm hungry... And..."

Without a second to waste, he frantically opened the plastic bottle he was given and drank all the water inside. Dirt and mud flew along the water, disgustingly. After finishing the whole bottle he looked scrunched up and completely unrecognizable, but the thirst was quenched. He looked at the building again, giant letters "B" and "R" stood above the wide doorframe with a lacking letter in the middle. He examined the dead phone as he walked inside, the sound of doors swinging and local music playing within breezing by the ears.

The music stopped once the locals saw him, things turned awkward pretty quickly. They whispered to each other, silently communicating and eyeing the unfortunate soul that found their way here. He sat down on a stool, a drop of sweat going by his forehead, he didn't know for what reason.

"What is it you want." The rusted bartender walked up to him, tossing their dryer on the table.

"Y'all got snacks?" He nervously, but quickly, spoke out loud amidst the silence.

The bartender gave him a judgmental eyebrow raise. "Snacks. How *offensive*, if you can't take on this." They muttered.

From beneath the table they brought out a whole giant raw leg of an animal, slightly toasted from the sun outside. For a moment, it looked like it jittered. He stared at it with an elongated, repulsed and wide-eyed face.

"No pay." They spoke directly to his face with a spit, "We'll see you take on our leaders' appetizer."

He was hungry, after all. He grabbed the bone with his two hands and went in for a bite. Struggling to pierce through the meat with his teeth, he struggled with it like as if it were rubber. Suddenly, someone tapped into his shoulder.

"Now who are you?" A small child asked, "What town do you come from?"

He took a moment to reflect at the mirror behind the bartender, for a moment it seemed like the whole world froze and went gray.

"Who am I?"

He looked at the meat he was attempting to take a bite out of, and all the people around. Unfriendly, savage, and dirty.

"What town do I come from?"

He kept asking himself, but deep in his mind was nothing but static and fog. But a voice echoed again, a faint, familiar voice, much like his.

He slammed his hands on the table. "Which of you was the one who dared to murder me!" They pointed at everyone, suddenly infuriated. Their audience was confused.

"Someone here thought it'd be wise to rob me of all my belongings! You musta not heard of the legends, you musta not heard of the feats from neighboring continents. Y'all don't wanna mess with the stars, I tell you that!" They walked around the bar, with confident and menacing slow struts, "...And YOU! Sweet summer child, I don't come from 'nowhere here." They took off their shirt, partly due to frustration and heat. They tossed the sweaty mess on the floor, "A topless hero, I've travelled through countless planets, sticking by the sides of leaders. My body is a blank canvas, colored by the people who have earned my dear trust..."

They sat back down on the stool, grabbed the leg, and took a proper bite out of it, "The name's Ally." They spat on the floor.

The people around them grew intrigued, suspicious, endeared.

"Tell us more." Someone from the back yelled out.

"Took ya long enough, seems like no one here is educated well enough." They got up, with the leg in their hands, "Many call me a ranger, ally, a topless maniac, I have many names! From planet to planet I fell, adapting to any environment and civilizations there are. I don't recall my origins, but they surely must've been tough!" They took another bite, gesturing with the leg as they spoke. "In the worlds where there is nothing but the sea, I fought whole krakens with my bare fists! Harpoons from the crew's ships filled the skies as we hunted beasts beyond your wildest imagination, the waves crashed down on us each second until the scourge was taken away. In planets where everything was just the skies we flew on giant birds, taking down colossal and corporate metal titans. Nature above all! As they say, throw me anywhere and I'll bring back a whole leg."

Someone around wasn't falling for the little stories, Ally noticed and walked up to them, "Are you doubting me, *sir*? I see it in your eyes, you know what we do with people like you from my places?"

They hit the pillar with the leg they were holding, full force. It made a squelching noise and a mess, "We hit 'em hard! Till none their organs ain't functioning! Then we cook 'em, and eat 'em for days! So you, I'd advise not to mess with me, got it?! One of y'all better introduce me to this place, having my belongings robbed right away sure is one first impression!"

And with that, Ally sat back down on the stool, "I'll have some water now." They told the bartender, confidently.

"W-We're running on reserves." They stuttered.

"Was I asking?" Ally leaned in closer to their face with a mean look.

"Right away." They nodded.

A small whiskey glass with water poured from a black bottle, they drank it all with a strong sip. "Thanks-"

The doors of the bar suddenly crashed open. The sun sept in from outside, a large shadow was formed from the silhouette that stood at the doorframe.

"A newcomer." Her deep voice and presence struck fear.

She crouched down, looked at the mess from the meat, sniffed it, "Eating my snack, making a mess with it. Whoever you are..."

She sat down on the stool next to Ally, who was frozen in fear. She was about twice the size of them, fuzzy, muscly. As if she slammed down on the table, with a her head resting on her hand, she examined Ally. 

"I'll have that now." She grabbed the whole unfinished leg with her single hand and took a large bite out of it. Her teeth were sharp, much like a wolf. "Care to repeat your stories again?" She ordered some alcohol to go along her stay, smoking a makeshift cigarette with tobacco seeping out.

"S-She fought giant sea creatures with her bare fists!"

"He flew on giant birds!"

"They have tons of stories to tell!"

"Huh." She looked at the people in the bar, "You made your mark already... Now tell me," She looked at them with a stare that felt like a stab to the chest, "Are we just making shit up now?"

There was a moment of silence between the two. Ally struggled to say something, so in their apparent best decision they grabbed the leg from her hand and ate it. Everyone gasped. She looked at them in complete disbelief. It was tough to swallow it all, took a minute.

The next second, Ally was flung crashing through the window out into the scorching suns. She stomped her way out of the bar, standing right above bruised Ally.

"Clock strikes one, we see each other right here! You are DEAD!"

At that moment, Ally could only groan as she walked away. A gun was thrown to them and prayers from others.
