The unforgiving suns blazed on the surface ever so more. The wind just slightly howled, hanging junk jingled to fill in the atmosphere with its noise. Everyone watched from their homes through the windows. They were either gonna see yet another unfortunate soul perish by their leader, or they were gonna see history being made. Ally stood at the middle, covered in hastily put bandages to cover their bruises from earlier. They were given copper brass knuckles and a pistol made of junk. This was not gonna be a typical duel. The leader stood far at the other side, needing no weapon but two chakrams to aid her wrath.

Ally took out the pistol's magazine, empty. They reached out to their belt to grab a handful of bullets and insert them in, but as they were doing it their twitchy and clumsy fingers made many of them fall onto the sand. 

The leader watched, completely unimpressed.

"Y'know what!" They shouted, "I don't even need a gun!"

Ally tossed the pistol out to their left side, readying their fists in a combat stance. The leader reacted with a convincing eyebrow raise.

She raised her fist, and with it three fingers.

"Three."

A notable pause occurred.

"Two."

One of the fingers went down.

"One."

But all of a sudden, deep rumbling was felt beneath their feet. She swiftly looked behind her, a trail of smoke was seen up in the sky. Without hesitation, she blazed off into the source of the smoke. Ally stood there, confused, alone. The earth stopped shaking.

"Well now." Ally started to proudly walk among the buildings, "I'll take that as an unannounced surrender, what a surprise! If y'alls leader is too afraid to mess with me, it may be about time to-"

All the windows in all the buildings were shut with a loud thud. Pillars emerged from their walls, digging deep into the earth. An intimidating alarm sounded for a moment.

"*Remain in your homes. Do not step outside. Wait until reinforcement is lifted.*"

A voice echoed through the speakers. The ground shook, much more fiercer this time around.

"*Remain in your homes. Do not step outside. Wait until reinforcement is lifted.*"

From the horizon, a thick worm about the size of a large vehicle emerged from the dry earth. Leaping dozens of meters up into the sky, preying on birds, even more erupted from the ground soon after. A whole pack of death worms. An amphibian for lunch.

They made a run for it, away from the incoming horde.

It wasn't long before the death worms found their way into the tunnels of the town. They emerged and leapt at Ally with their heads being nothing but mouths full of fangs and a disgusting long tongue. Somehow, Ally managed to avoid all of them through rancid evasive maneuvers and lots of yelps and yells for help. The death worms fought over them like a pack of starved animals, biting and spiting into each other to thin out the competition. Its blood and toxic spit covered them completely. Ally found a bike, everything they ever needed for the current moment. They kicked into the pedals and rode with all their strength away from the town. The worms dove into the earth, causing it to rumble immensely.

Meanwhile,

"Is that..."

Somewhere in an enclosed outpost, with screens showing various broadcasts of security cameras and data on the environment. The leader stared very closely at one peculiar screen.

"What the hell is he doing?!"

Ally rode the bicycle for their dear life. Followed by worms who kept submerging in and out of the earth like dolphins in a sea. One of the worms' tongues got wrapped around the back wheel of their bike, and with a powerful stop it managed to tear it off. Their whole bike flipped over backwards and they were fell and slid down a long natural slope. They hit a cactus with their whole body and a yelp, likely one of the sharpest kinds around. With exhausted grunts, nature and gravity got Ally unstuck and they continued to tumble down. When they hit the lowest point, they raised their head and saw Yzahela playing a guitar in a dark cave with her pet camel.

She stopped and looked at Ally, "Oh! How's your first day going?" She asked kindly with a smile.

Ally looked at her, floored, beaten up and battered with cacti needles and pieces stuck on their skin.

"Yeah, good luck with that." She nodded.

As they tried to crawl towards her, a death worm's tongue wrapped their leg and dragged them out into the suns. Yzahela continued to play the guitar as if nothing had happened.

One of the worms had them in their mouth, traversing the rough sands of the surface like a snake. Ally writhed and struggled in its grasp as it was accelerating faster, and faster, until it hit something really hard with its head. A loud metal thud, the worm knocked itself unconscious. Carefully, Ally slipped away from the worm's fangs. They took out all the needles, pieces of cacti, and looked back at what the death worm hit.

A giant white pipe which extended out into unimaginable lengths, acting like borders for the land. They came closer to it with a struggled walk, and heard something that sounded like water flowing within. A strange green symbol was obscured by dirt, with a swipe they uncovered it and...

"Dawn of the Green Light." They slowly read it out loud, "Working towards your better future."

"*W-We're running on reserves.*" They recalled the bartender's words.

"Hm." Ally looked at the unmoving body of the death worm, then at the town which surprisingly wasn't that far off. The pipes didn't seem to connect to the town whatsoever. They touched their own wounds, which were already healed with a surprise, though very itchy. The ground shook again, which meant a call for them to get moving again. They survived the first beating by pure luck, but they were confident enough to survive another.
With a struggled decision they glanced at the worm's teeth. The smell was incredibly repulsive, and it could wake up at any given moment, but after some hasty self reassuring they stuck their hand into its mouth and tore off a tooth. Somehow, the worm did not wake up, but its gum bled and Ally jittered at the sight too much that they just ended up running away from it. They'd proceed to find a small stick that seemed durable enough, and with dead long leaves they wrapped the tooth on the stick to make a small knife. Having known how to do these things, it felt like a voice in the back of their head guided them from past lessons. They cut off the arms' of the cacti around and stuck them to even more dead sticks, once they seemed sufficient enough they strapped them onto their belt for expendable use. For the final thing to do they'd choose to start fire. Conveniently, they were in hell, they brought a bunch of flammable objects out on the rocky surface basked in the two suns and waited. Nothing happened... but they took out the brass knuckles from their back pocket to force a tiny, concentrated point of light on the top stick using reflections. At last, smoke, and then fire.

They were now ready, with a confident smirk they looked back at the town. They fully believed they went through worse things in their past, and this was going to be yet another one of their stories to tell everyone.

That all fell apart the moment they encountered another death worm on their way back. They dropped the torch and ran for their life once more. Their numbers seemed more sparse this time around, once Ally made it to the town's square there was no other place to run. They had to do something about the worm. They threw and tossed the cacti on the sticks they'd have fabricated moments earlier. Obviously, they did absolutely nothing to the desert-hardened monsters of the wasteland! The worm spit out its tongue to wrap Ally's stomach, but in a triumphant move they quickly took out the dagger they made and sliced the tongue off before it could grab them into its mouth. At the moment of the worm's weakness, Ally found a steel rod and shoved it into its mouth. It seemed to start choking and move erratically, they took it as a sign to shove even more junk into its mouth. The residents of the town watched through small openings in awe. At last, the beast suffocated to death, and Ally stood there, panting.

They yelled out from their inner feel of victory, making rude gestures mocking the dead death worm.

"Did y'see that?"
"I cannot believe it."
"He really took on the pack of death worms all by himself."
"Doesn't she know she could just hide in a cave?"
"Have you not listened?"

"She is the topless maniac."

The ground suddenly rumbled and another death worm erupted from the ground, catching and swallowing Ally in an instant... But its feast was abruptly interrupted by a loud gunshot. The leader arrived again, holding a handheld cannon in her arms and fired at the worm relentlessly with the help of someone close to reload. It wasn't long before that worm also fell dead, the leader walked up to its center and with a chakram they sliced the skin and meat off. Once exposed, Ally laid in the organs and disgusting slime as the leader looked at them with an impressed look.

She looked down away from them, thinking, "Ally..."
