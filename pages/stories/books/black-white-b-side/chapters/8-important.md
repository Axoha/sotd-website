Two whole days have passed since the wastelanders' siege on the factory.

Ally slowly wakes up. Accompanied by the faint repeating sound of a heart rate monitor. Covered by comfy ragged blankets, with tubes from several bags of fluid attached to their body. Just squinting from the bright yellow light which lit the room, "Hey!" Someone familiar dressed in a long white coat waved at them.

"O..." Ally struggles to speak.

A person just right across the room was typing quickly on a thick laptop, closing it to swiftly focus on her. "You woke up! You actually woke up!"

"Otto..." She kept squinting with her eyes, the world was coming back to her with each second.

"Oh, she's going to be so ecstatic about this." Otto walked back and forth, quickly getting something from the sink, "Thirsty?"

A cup of pristine clear water was offered to her, Ally drooled at the sight. She kept regaining senses and control, and slowly she took the cup and drank all the water in it quickly.

"If you need more, don't be afraid to ask." He smiled, "Things around here have really changed while you were sleeping, well, I couldn't really participate in what they were doing. Your body was in such a mess that I was stressing out over all the time." She chuckled, "It's good to have you back."

Ally looked at herself.

From her sense of touch, she felt stitches, many stitches, differences. Her body was slightly more curvier, her chest was slightly bigger, her skin was smooth, not counting the scars of course. Her chest especially had a large scar, still stitched together, she could only remember when Surl tore it apart.

"While working on your body..." Otto spoke up, "Your body was so torn apart I couldn't get it back to its original state. I worked with everything I had, I was so unsure about everything... Does being in that body feel normal to you?"

Ally nodded with a warm smile, it was still hard for her to speak up, her throat felt raspy.

"Phew." Otto rubbed the sweat off from his forehead, "I'll still need to take care of a few things, is that okay with you?"

"...Yeah." She replied with a damaged voice, but her feelings were healthy.

Otto sat right next to her on the bed, getting her to sit upright for something important. He wrapped the chest of her body with bandages. It needed to be wrapped as tightly as possible due to risks of it tearing apart. After some minor check ups and pills, she was healthy enough to be let go with a crutch. While walking through the hallway, she couldn't help but notice empty pots. There were many, unnaturally by each door.

She stepped outside, the suns unforgivingly shining on the town square as if nothing had changed. She felt hungry, maybe she shouldn't have lied about that to Otto, or maybe she didn't remember, but instinctively she went into the one place she knew the best for food. 

The BR.

The doors swung as she entered through the doorframe. It was filled with people, she immediately assumed it was noon. The music stopped, everyone turned back to look at her, things turned awkward quite quickly. Emma was at the center busy snacking on a prepared leg, until she also turned back - and her jaw dropped.

"...Ally." She muttered.

Ally gulped in response.

She swiftly stood up and tackled Ally with a hug, "You missed out on the entire raid, you jackass!" She said with laughter and tears coming from her eyes.

Ally fell into the soft embrace of her hug. "Hey." She kept trying to speak with her hurt voice.

"Oh, you've missed out on so much. Everyone! Tell her how it went down."

And so the music continued. Ally was carefully led to her seat and was told everything with expressive gestures and energy.

After one successful call, the other leaders arrived to help. The Demolisher hijacking, or better put, Ally's stupid blind optimism, sparked just enough hope for the towns to come. Emma was found way sooner than Ally, she was brought into Johnny's walker by Otto and put in a place where the locals described as 'place where she somehow recovered quickly'. She was anxious, like terribly anxious about Ally. The Third walker suffered the most damage out of the whole ordeal, but its people survived with many injuries and scars. Without the highest leader to command the Green Light's factory, they were uncoordinated and confused. The wastelanders seized this opportunity with six giant walkers and trampled over the whole place with relative ease, though, three out of the six suffered major damages from the factory's armaments. They freed the water, stole technology that's rightfully theirs, and couldn't help but wonder what came up to this point. Most questioned what happened with Ally, the one who completely steered the plan into a successful plan, and the Green Light's leader, who just disappeared. The search began and soon after Ally and the leader were both found dead unconscious. Otto decided to take on his most stressful and hardest job ever, which was to take care of Ally until she could stand up again.

Ally was just so relieved they won, though she understood that this was just the beginning of a war. She wouldn't think negatively of it, she was optimistic about the people.

Using the Green Light's prebuilt infrastructure, anyone could get as much water as they needed.

"Oh, by the way." Emma continued, "Do you know what day it is?"

Ally tilted her head with question.

"Happy birthday, dude!" She cheered for her, "We saw it on your ID card and we were like wait that's perfect. So nice that you woke up on this day too, I was so hoping you would! Didn't Otto tell you already?!"

"N-no." She answered.

"That damn guy, keeps forgetting about whatever." She jokingly spoke with scorn, "He's prepared something for you in your room, says it's from the raid, claims it took a lot of tinkering, bla-bla... You could go check it out right now, we'll be here if you want to hang around, or you could stay here and check it our later, you do you!" She burst into laughter.

"I'll see, I still need to recover." She spoke honestly.

"That's cool, see ya later!" She waved, and so did everyone else.

Walking back to her room had Ally realize the damage the walker had sustained. What a shame the greenhouse shattered, at least it still operates.

In her room, on her bed, Sue's plush toy and various other personal belongings were laid down on the silky sheets. Some of the stood out however, a DVD and a note.

"Hey, Otto here."

"If you're reading this, happy birthday to you! I understand things were extremely complicated the past few days, I can't imagine how convoluted it must've been for you. We found out a lot after *he* arrived, but we didn't really care much for it. We only look at the present and future, the past is something we just faintly look back on, and occasionally celebrate. Using your name we found a lot of personal information in Green Light's database, no one looked at them don't worry, but I downloaded what seemed important and burned it on a DVD for you to browse through. Maybe the past is something you despise, or it's something you would like to look back on. I figured you were suffering from amnesia once I had you as a patient for the first time. What you do with all your past belongings is completely up to you, but... everyone's happy for who you are, especially after what you've done for us."

"-Doc"

The message warmed her heart, she went through everything else first, reliving memories as she organized and put them wherever it fit. Once only the DVD remained on the bed, she stared at it for a solid moment.
She put the DVD in a drawer, took her crutch, and walked out of the room to go hang around with her newfound family.
