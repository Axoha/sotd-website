I walk through the desolate underwater wasteland. No one is here, just me and my pet fish. Each step leaves a trail in the sand, I often find myself looking up at the surface of the water. The sun is shining up above, but the light doesn't reach the depths I am in. I don't feel any extra emotion, just whatever I feel when I'm asked the question, who am I.

"*How come you did not find your answer yet?*" A giant fish with big eyes just like mine speaks to me.

"I don't know..." I respond to it, holding my right arm.

"*A word.*" It speaks with its muffled, booming voice, "*Hear the word...*"

What sounded like glass shattering woke Ally up from sleep. She instinctively checks the digital clock next to her bed, one in the morning. She was sat upright in the bed sheets, looking around, just pure darkness and silence, though a sound like thud came through the walls. She got up to investigate, quietly and slowly. The darkness and silence was making her anxious, really anxious. With careful movements she gets out into the hallway, trying to walk slowly without making any noise, though the sound of a foot shuffling through the floor slipped by. She treaded on, sweating bullets over the sight of what seemed to be an endless dark hallway. Another muffled thud came through the walls, she managed to track it well enough to find the source. A door to a room which read "Emma".

With curiosity and repressed fear, she gently turned the knob and opened the door.

She screamed until a beastly hand swiftly clamped her mouth shut and with a pinch turned it into a closed zipper. The more Ally looked at its face, the more it seemed like the beast's eyes multiplied with each second with her large jagged mouth growing larger and larger. She squirmed, muffling into the zipper, trying to fight for her dear life in the beasts' grasp, but it was futile. The beast consumed her.

"*A word.*" The fish repeated its words with its muffled, booming voice, "*Hear the word...*"

The fish turned into a giant, black silhouette reminiscent of a bear.

"Empty. Your head is so empty..." And it ran away upon seeing the thing from behind Ally.

The beast, with an angsty pull took out two mystic chakrams from its back, and proceeded to perform a spiritual dance. Ally stood at the middle of the reflective black floor with the source of light being a full moon right above her head. With every step the beast took in its dance pulsated the floor with color. Red, orange, yellow, green, it was fast. The sight of the beast was enough to send Ally into a trance. It wasn't long enough until the whole dimension turned into vibrant sparks of color. The beast came closer and closer to Ally, dancing around her, before suddenly snatching and pulling out something from her chest. Drums began playing along a hypnotic fast beat.

".eM htiW enO emoceB मेरे साथ एक हो जाओ."

Her vessel collapsed into dust and disappeared into nothingness, all that remained was her wandering soul. Fuzzy on the inside. The beast took her hand and suddenly its form became clearer. It was Emma, in a beautiful traditional form, making hypnotizing moves with her two chakrams. She moved like the waves, and soon enough her moves transmitted to Ally's soul too. She lend her one of the chakrams, and then the dot at the center of her forehead glowed more. The backgrounds went from spirals, to color explosions, to just pulsating colors in darkness.

".yllA ,uoY ekiL I मुझे तुम पसंद हो, एली।"

Faint statues, synchronized in their moves danced at the background. Their eyes shined with white, six dots on each head, and a seventh on the forehead.

The background scenery shift into the universe. They continued dancing on an invisible floor, way closer to each other now. Rainbow strings waved in the universe, pulsating to the beat of the music.

"...yllA ,eM teG uoY deveileR oS mA I मुझे बहुत राहत मिली कि तुम मुझे समझती हो, एली।"

"...And I Am So Glad You Now Understand Me, Ally."

They were even closer now, they both felt the last stages coming.

"Who Are You To Be Such A Good Dancer, Ally?"

Suddenly the world exploded in a shining white light.

"*Wake up now, c'mon.*" Was the first thing Ally heard as her ears rang and vision opened up.

Otto was seen pointing a really strong flashlight directly into Emma's groaning face. Ally looked around, they were at the walker's back with its numbingly bright stadium lights turned on them.

"Hey, Ally, you good?" Otto casually turned back to her.

She looked at herself, dressed in some sort of traditional clothing. She was completely without words.

"Yeah, first time isn't ever easy to process." He stopped flashing the light at Emma's face, "Never expected you to find out so soon, you're a curious type eh? I guess this is what happens when you sleep in a room next to hers."

"...What even happened?" Ally finally spoke, holding her head. Her mind hurt a lot.

After Emma's shadow got off her, she looked very flustered, and swiftly made a leave without a word.

"Emma has a unique quirk..." Otto sat down across her, "Each night, it's like something awakens within her and she turns into a monster that can be described only as a ever-changing black silhouette. It's really bizarre." He gestured with his hands to express his feelings about it, "One time the local hospital I lived in was completely destroyed by death worms, and so I was assigned to live in the walker until it is rebuilt. A month later I fell into the same trap you did! It was the most terrifying night of my life... When the next day finally came she just held my shoulders and told me to never tell anyone."

Ally thought back to her psychedelic experience, it felt oddly romantic.

"Even despite everything, I was interested in it. No book ever mentioned something even remotely close to this, other than drugs, I guess. She agreed to me studying her on agreed times. I got really beat by her monstrous form at times, but eventually I figured out light wears the darkness off..."

She looked back at the entrance to the walker, thinking thoughts.

"...And, Ally, I need to ask urgently since this never happens, what did you see? What did she do to you?" He asked with peak curiosity.

"Doc, I think I'd rather go back to sleep, sorry." She politely rejected him.

"Oh? Oh, okay, yeah that's fair. Maybe another time then." He was visibly disappointed.

One day until the water reserves run out.

Ally wakes up at noon, greeted by the usual scalding hot temperatures. There was no water to drink around, she wanted to get some, but thought back to a conversation about using water thoroughly throughout the day. Yesterday she was given a Miswak by Otto, a tree root to brush her teeth with. As she looked in the mirror she struggled, but precisely remembered the steps they told her about and after some fighting, it went smoothly. With a knock the door to her room suddenly opened.

"Hey." Emma opened up, "You ready for the meeting?"

"What meeting?" She answered with a slice of bread in her mouth.

"The plan for tomorrow." She added.

"Right." Ally's eyes drifted away for a second, "Yeah, let's do it."

Together they walked outside to the main square of the town. Just awkward silence between them two, Emma hitched a ride on a camel wearing various trinkets and Ally hopped on its back right behind her. The meeting spot was supposedly far, they both put on damp clothes over their heads to shield from the heat.

"Ally..." Emma picked up the courage to speak, "Don't tell anyone about what happened last night, ok?"

She was surprised to hear her acknowledge it, "I won't." She nodded.

"I'll tell you more after the meeting, " Their conversation from there drifted into something more casual.

Here they arrived at the great glass dome. Where the seven leaders of each town meet to discuss important necessities in one of the last standing greenhouses of the continent. It was just them, and Ally.
