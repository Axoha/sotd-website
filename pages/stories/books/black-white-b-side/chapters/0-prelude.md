The mechanical noise and whirrs of a ship filled the empty and dark room.

*My dear first entry,*

*Today I was finally tasked with a job. Though it's one of the lowest ones, I finally get to work in a place I've always dreamt of being in. My older brother, Surl, used to always take me here when I was little. Space is so wonderful, isn't it? The machines I got to see, the worlds and stars I never thought I'd reach in my life. He told me all about them, all about his job, and he'd always hope I'd be right by his back when I grow up. Such a shame our sister wasn't old enough to come with us, but I'll bring her for sure when I can. This is for you, Surly. I'm coming after all this time.*

"So relieving to be finally out of that boring ol' dump." He kept thinking to himself, not knowing how to continue the entry. The green pen ticks against his head in a repeated motion, trying to get his own mind to work. He looks out through the window...

*Stars. I'll visit each one of them. Send postcards from each civilized planet. You were really lacking with that! I'll become the greatest ranger ever out there, with dozens of stories to tell. I'll sail the seas of aquatic earths, fist-fighting whole krakens against all the odds. I'll conquer the scorching-hot deserts and bring life to the dead rivers. I'll fly through the breezy skies and hunt down enormous birds for dinner. I will climb the ranks, and I will beat your spot eventually. You better be ready.*

He smiled as he wrote, letting their creativity loose on the blank pages of his personal notebook. "I want snacks." They stopped writing, "Good thing there's a stop around this planet, sure don't mind the luxuries I've been getting. Am I right lads?"

No one else was in the ship. Just him, his younger sisters' plush toy, and the autopilot.

"I see how it is." They scooted over to the terminal on the wall, "Imma buy tons of snacks, I'm starving."

"Hello!-"

"Don't care 'bout your feelings pilot." He swiped the autopilot's dialogue off of the touch screen before proceeding to tap and type to modify the trajectory of the ship.

"Warn-"

"Still don't care." He blew a bubble from his mouth using some of the gum he has been chewing for almost an hour.

"You!" He snapped to the plush toy, sat right across him, "Make the last press!"

He swiftly grabbed the plushie and brought it over to the terminal, and with a goofier voice he spoke for it, "But there are warnings from the pilot! Don't you remember dummy, unverified detours are against the rules and are super dangerous!"

"That's what they all say." He posed like a confident hero, "You always worry too much, sister, we'll be taking this quick detour for the snacks! Whatever they all say, our stomachs can't wait any longer! We'll be on our divine feast 'till we land!" And so, he made the plush tap the confirm button.

He sat back down with the plushie in his hands.

"It's quiet here." He looked through the window again, "If only you were here, Sue. Who am I gonna talk to now?"

He breathed on the window and started doodling stars with his finger.

"Nah." He smiled, "I'll assert myself there this time..."

A small shining beam of light appeared at the horizon.

"...And I'll make myself be known! Everyone will know my-"

From an unknown source, the ship was hit severely by a beam. Split in two right away, his suit activated instantly, the planet's orbit slowly took the ship down onto its surface. Through the numbing ringing noise that came from the impact he wailed in panic, but even despite everything he tried to grab onto his belongings. He clung onto large chunks of debris scattered from the ship, spinning and going out into the unknown. He reached out to his bag, only for his head to be hit really hard by an aimless steel plate.
