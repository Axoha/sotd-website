On the year of 2760, five years before the monochrome era came to be, a research team by the name of 'Omega' consisting of five people went on an expedition to examine an anomaly in space. Bill, the genetically engineered giant to mimic the gauntlet found decades ago. Rose, the main researcher, the Citadelarian working with the Babylon in secret before it was made public. Warnie, the safety and rescue operative. Houston, the rookie, recently hired for extravagant skill... and finally, there was Lars. Together they manned a guardian-class ship with the name and designation of 'Sweetie' *SPS-999*, some of the most secure and elegant vehicles coming from the New Babylon. That day, five years ago, they became the first to enter the 'underworld', they became the first to meet the Inka, they became the first to witness the second prophecy. The end, all it read, as follows:

"...The prophecy fails, the worlds end."

Alarm clock, beep beep boop boop bap bap. Back to the year 2765, a week after the monochrome era ended. The research team still lives on, done celebrating their finished prophecy mission. The worlds did not end, all is fine. It was going to be another lazy day, there was nothing to do.

"Oh, Houston, There you are!" Bill speaks up, relaxed on a chair, "I've been meaning to ask, seriously."

"What is it?" Houston turns their head to him, busy making coffee.

"Why wasn't I told before that I shouldn't have been the one to wield the gauntlet in the first place!!!" That friendly gesture quickly turned into anger, "I just remembered, seriously, it doesn't make sense! Why did I waste my energy, and literally the time of our defenders, to fly after a damn dummy?! Why did my stomach need to get blown!"

Houston quickly turned their head away, holding in their laughter, "Damn you're slow-"

"Slow?! I may be slow?! Tell me why seriously!" He got up to look down at Houston. With a snatch he grabbed the neck of Houston's shirt and raised him from the floor.

"It's just... so... funny-" Small tears were slowly streaming down from their eyes, and then they just exploded in laughter. 

"What am I looking at, what is going on." Ferdinand walked into the living room, just slightly confused. He no longer had the great golden left arm, instead, it was now a durable silver prosthetic.

"This twig isn't telling me why!" He let go of Houston.

"Okay, okay." Houston took a deep breath, "Picture this. Ferd could've done a lot of damage with that gauntlet, and just in case of that we'd have you, not holding back due to him being chosen."

"That's... stupid." Bill's anger turned to confusion.

"Bro, you should be glad. The gauntlets suck." Ferdinand interrupted their conversation, "Those special wrists, which you can charge to deal devastating blows are far cooler, trust me."

"Lame! The gauntlet looks good, feels good, is capable of very insane things-"

"You do not want *it*." He looked directly at his eyes.

"Fine." They looked at each other, two identical giants holding their eye contacts.

"Gay staredown." Houston interrupts their competition.

"No no no!" Bill covers their face, "It's nothing like that!"

"Anyway..." They look at their coffee, which has just finished, "Where's Rose at?"

Ferdinand shrugs, Bill is incapacitated. "Probably depressed in their lab again, wouldn't be a surprise." They thought to themselves, "I do wonder what the whole plan with that Citadel Vitamin Corp was... We had to cut the Citadel off after that, being so into war is not what the Babylon stands for. The Genkatan... the unstable matter... what a mess, what was he thinking?"

In that moment of deep thought, a comet appears in the orange sky as the sun rose... and in the next second, the alarms blared and the highest threat-level was called.

**??? - UNIDENTIFIED SUDDEN COMET**

- Scary to look at.
- Not green.
- Approaching at rapid speeds!

Two guardian-class ships were deployed from the moon's main stations, their thrusters blazing right away to reach and investigate the comet. They kept a secure connection with the New Babylon, and once the comets' trajectory was fully calculated, no time was wasted. By this point, the comet has already struck and pierced right through one of the Babylon's many barriers that surrounded their systems. The two guardian ships approached the comet covering each side, left and right, investigating... until it instantly changed its trajectory to ram and destroy the ship on its left.

It was clear, this was no comet.

The guardian ship which remained on its right went to full throttle, the thrusters blazed and left sparkling dust in the emptiness of space. In urgency, it got ahead of the... *thing*, and deployed three control units strapped with wire and chain connected to the ship. Those control units were unspoken of, secret, exclusive to guardian-class ships. They were unintelligent robots equipped with man-made copies of the gauntlet, they swung at the back of the ship identifying the anomaly that was behind them.

"It is the chosen subject with the gauntlet, Ferd Ray." A status update comes by.

"THE KING?! WASN'T IT OVER?!" Bill slams the desk of the control room in disbelief.

"Seems, the gauntlet remains!" The crew nervously replies, "What do we do?! Our friends were just destroyed!!!"

The silhouette of the flash became more and more clear as time went on. The King, Ferd, with his four wings and the stark black gauntlet flew through space right behind the guardian ship. The control units begun their defense, controlling the artificial gauntlets to erase whatever creation Ferd would have used against the ship. They swung, left and right, connected to the ship mimicking each move Ferd did to form an impenetrable wall. 

"H-He must be led out of this system! Right now, get on that!" Bill nervously commanded, sweating.

"The beginning of the end." Houston looked down, shaking their head, "I know what he's after, we won't stop him no matter what experimental divine technology we throw at him."

"Huh? What do you mean?!" Bill turned to Houston.

"Whatever he desires, he'll get to it. Stubborn and reckless." They clenched their fists, "Y'know, maybe about time we get what we truly deserve!"

Through sheer brute force, Ferd managed to cling onto the guardian ship despite heavy fire. He ignored the control units, he knew there was no way to take them out. With the gauntlet he clawed at the fragile metal surface, taking out a huge chunk before backing away. With that plate, indestructible in the eyes of the control units due to being property of the Babylon, he'd use it to slice off the wire that connected them to the ship. 

Ferdinand looked at the live footage, reminiscing of his past. 

*"Remember, focus on the golden arm."*

*"I pray for the best for each of you."*

The words rang out in his head from someone he overheard, leading a charge of five people equipped with gauntlets.

*"We beg of you to remain calm, please."*

*"We almost got it off!!!"*

And those, that were the victim's last words he heard. From the times when he used to be a great titan, where worlds were nothing but obstacles, where violence was simply everything. That uneasy feeling, it felt like as if it were all coming back.

Rose's experimental machines were deployed. Ferd finally struck down the second guardian-class ship and flew directly into the surface of the New Babylon's earth. Piercing right through the clouds, the atmosphere, a mechanical worm found its way to clasp him with its teeth to snatch him away from the planet - Rose's finest. Spiky, edgy, golden, lots of sawblades, lots of eyes, the worm flew in the air using jets, slowly wiggling its way up like a dragon into space. More ships from the Babylon were deployed from the moon's space station, any kind.

But even then... it wasn't enough. With a bright flash, all communication was cut with what and who was sent. It was over, there was nothing the Babylon could do, but even despite the pathetic odds they fired and fought. Ferd ignored the fire, instead, he dove right onto one specific continent, one specific country, one specific city, one specific building. Piercing through the thick clouds again, he crashed into the museum and through many walls within. It was at such a speed no defense mechanism could've caught on, not even the fastest missiles or bullets. He got up, brushed off the dust from his fur, and walked through the smoke.

"Where is the doctor, engineer, Rose." He talked, even through the smoke he could sense the people around.

"W-What the hell!!!" Bill coughed on the floor, "You caused all this just to see him?!"

"He was behind it all, each one of you, but especially him!" His voice cracked, "I'll destroy this whole palace of yours if I don't get to murder this maniac!"

The smoke was still thick, Houston rather looked away from his voice in shame. Ferdinand stood up, without saying a word, he dashed into Ferd to throw one mean punch with his new left silver prosthetic. Ferd sensed the attack, obviously, he grabbed the fist of the mechanical arm with his gauntlet, but as Ferdinand predicted he used the small window of opportunity to use his right fist to hit his stomach, hard. The pain from such force was very real, Ferd recoiled and lost sense as Ferdinand headbutted his chest to ram him right into a wall. He made sure to pin the arm of his gauntlet against the wall the hardest.

"Listen." He looked at Ferd with scary eyes, grunting, "There is no point, stop this."

"Like the hell would I stop now?!" He struggled to respond, trying to reach an arm out to attack.

"I can help you get that gauntlet off of you. You need to stop, before things go terribly wrong." His eyes turned more concerned as Ferd struggled more.

"I don't care about that anymore! Let me be!" He managed to force his power into a kick, sending Ferdinand back to the floor with great injury. "Just, let me be satisfied for once!" He got off the wall, looking at his healing wounds and walking away.

"Try harder than that!" Ferdinand got up, holding his stomach, "Do you not have friends? A literal family?! What do you think they'll see in you after you commit more of these atrocities!"

"Getting so personal." Ferd shook.

"I've lived through this, I'm not going to let you go down like I did." He clenched his fists.
