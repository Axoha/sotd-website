As the first snowflakes fell on the wasteland, far from any conflict, atop Ally's moving walker... A familiar season was coming. One which is colorful, beautiful. A week later after the expedition, Ferd, Ally, and Houston have gone completely absent without a sign. No more war, no more politics, just the trio and the grand resource mover walker that became their home.

"Strange, it never snows here."

The door to a repurposed room creaked open. Ally came through with light coming from the hallways to Houston, their new room cold and dark.

"Hey." She brushed the back of her hair with a concerned, worried face, "Would you be up for a chat?"

Houston looked at her, then away. They were laying on the makeshift bed, clothed.

"If you don't want to talk, then that's okay. It's just been... days." She hung over the door, the light and her shadow cast over Houston. "Masy made you a sandwich, I'll leave it at the door when it's done."

"There's so much." They spoke with a weak voice, "Please don't leave."

Their response surprised Ally, finally. "Houston?" She walked into the room, settling down on the bed.

"I was just thinking about it all." There was a clear frown on their face, "I keep putting pressure onto you, I keep ruining everything, I-"

"Hey." She placed a hand on their shoulder, "Don't think about yourself like that."

"But it's... true." They spoke in a sigh.

"You're here." She smiled, "That's all that matters."

Finally they looked at her, and seeing her smile, it's as if all the terrible thoughts went numb even just for a moment. Her hand slid off from their shoulder to an offering, her hand out to help them get up from the bed. 

"C'mon, Houst, let's get you back."

And their hand slowly came to hold hers.

They trailed off far from the resource mover, somewhere along the shallow snow during the night. Quiet, silence, just the wind and their small, uncommon chatter.

"I present to you..." Ally anticipated something, somewhere right behind bushes and trees, "Drumrolls, please..."

Houston went ahead to uncover the leaves. Behind the foliage was a grand bubbly lake which emanated pink light from its depth. 

"*𝒯𝒽𝑒 𝓁𝒶𝓀𝑒 𝓉𝒽𝒶𝓉 𝓂𝒶𝓀𝑒𝓈 𝓎𝑜𝓊 𝓈𝓂𝑒𝓁𝓁 𝑔𝑜𝑜𝒹.*" She proudly presented with theatrical gestures.

"The lake that makes you- huh?!" They were confused.

"It's a natural bath, dummy!" She teased, "My personal favorite, you can just go for a swim here and you'll come out super fresh and clean! You don't even need to be naked, mother nature knew what they were doing with this one."

"We came all this way to..."

"One rule of starting anew, always begin with a bath!" She walked ahead of Houston through the bushes, "Someone close taught me that, and it's true."

"It's so cold out... I don't even have appropriate swimming gear..." Even from imagining being wet and the wind blowing, they shivered.

"Nothing's wrong with just going in with your clothes either, place also serves as a natural laundry machine." She smiled with a glee as she was ready to jump into the lake from a small board.

Houston stood near the water unsure whether to step in or not. Ally jumped in without taking off anything, not even her boots. The cold didn't bother her at all. She swam really quickly, swaying her body from side to side.

"The water's fine!" She yelled out from a distance after she surfaced from her first dive.

Houston found their courage to step into the water. Once it has soaked both their feet, a stinging cold feel hit them. The water was far from fine, it was cold, really cold, but despite the unpleasant feel they stayed. Suddenly, without a warning, Ally surfaced closer to them and grabbed their hands to drag them into the water. Houston's mind rushed with various negative thoughts, how the water would affect their eyes, how they couldn't swim, how the water was too cold, what living beings could be in the water. Their body went into a closed shock with panicked closed eyes and a shut mouth, but after a moment, they opened their eyes to a blurry vision of jellyfish swimming in the surprisingly deep depths of the lake. The water was just fine, their eyes didn't hurt. In that moment, just looking at the jellyfish move and the pink soft light beneath them, everything seemed fine. Their breath was running short, with slightly erratic movements they surfaced to gasp for air.

"How is it?" Ally swam up to them, the bottom half of her face underwater.

"The water is..." Houston's movement slowed down as they kept afloat, the wind that blew on their head was cold, but otherwise, "Warm, comfortable."

"There it is!" She smiled, with wide open arms.

And together they continued to swim through the bubbly lake. Swimming with clothes was slightly limiting, Ally had no fear in just taking off her shoes in the water and tossing them over to the gravel shore. After a long while, they both got out of the water all wet and sat down on the rocky surface. The wind blew, it was really cold. Ally tossed a drying towel over to Houston who immediately wrapped themself with it. Ally put most of her stuff on a drying rack nearby, though, she quickly realized it wasn't a good idea because they'd freeze up overnight.

"I-I think I'm gonna c-catch a cold." Houston stammered, shaking.

"You'll get over it." She sat down next to Houston, "Was it fun?"

"It was pretty fun, yeah." They replied, softly.

"That's cool." She smiled, again.

"Aren't you cold?" They asked, noticing Ally had nothing over her.

"Nah." She looked at herself, "I like the cold."

The moon shined over the lake in a crescent form. They both looked at the stars, the whole beautiful outer space. Leaves rustled, the wind blew with no mercy.

"Houst, I've been meaning to ask..." She looked at them, "Who is Lars? That caretaker called their name back then when talking to you, it's been running in my mind ever since."

"Lars..." Houston turned to her, touched by the name, "He was... my partner..." They struggled to find words, a frown formed on their face.

"I had someone like that, too." She looked back at the stars, "Fearless, reckless, she was my everything. Every full moon we'd go out together and roam around the places of home. She'd show me wonders, magic, love."

"Larmie." They added, "They were... lovely, brave, caring."

"Emma gave her life to me as a guardian." She placed her hands on her chest, "It's a constant reminder she was always here with me, the scar, she gave me a second chance at life and I'll be forever grateful."

"That guardian who gave its life to you... was her?" Houston was softly surprised, "I..." They raised their shirt, revealing a similar scar on their chest.

"Lars gave their life to you? He was a guardian?" She pointed, slightly shocked.

"Well... Not exactly. No." They looked away, pulling their shirt back. "We got into this real mess with the caretaker, but we escaped, and Rose... he was meant to fix us up, but apparently Larmie was so beyond fixing that only I came out alive. He wasn't a guardian, yet..." They open their palm to conjure a familiar flower, "I can do things like this ever since."

"Maybe there was something he never told you." She looked back at the stars, "Emma once told me she was brought into a strange dream to play a game of chess. Somehow, despite not being strategic at all, she won with two knights against a queen in a surrender by the opponent. I've been thinking about that too, with the whole prophecy unveiled in the Cathedral... You knew about it already, didn't you?"

"I suppose I never directly said it." They let out a sigh, "I was put in the role of a jester, who was named to ensure the prophecy goes right alongside the knight and the king. Even through the pain I had to separate the queen from Ferd. I had to do everything to ensure nothing goes Masy's way. I had to kill the caretaker, but, I couldn't bring myself to. We were constantly told the worlds will end if the prophecy fails, I'm- I'm scared Ally. I'm sorry for everything I've done."

Ally took a moment to think about it, "It's okay, dummy. Masy assured that everything'll be fine." She smiled, "You made the right choice, I hope Ferd understands your view well."

In that moment, Houston's outlook on the future seemed brighter. Finally, a piece of optimism shed down onto their mind.

And so time passes by, in Ally's grand walker that they called home, waiting for a message from the Inka. Ally celebrates Christmas for the first time with Houston, Ferd, and Masy, sharing gifts and celebrating love. New Years, fireworks are set off as the snow thickens on the surface, alcohol and fizzy drinks are served in hopes for a beautiful new year. The Disobedient's call to rid the Genkatan is ignored without Ferd knowing. The climate worsens, the walker freezes to the point of not being able to move. It seemed like as if Ferd was getting better and better with each day that passed, as if Ena wasn't his whole world anymore. January passes, February passes with the day of love, and at last, March. Masy was gone for two days, and she returns only to Houston at the middle of the night. The snow has engulfed most of the planet's surface, and it was not only their home that suffered through such a change.

"Houston." Masy stumbled through the darkness towards them, "Houston..."

They wake up, "...Hm? Oh, hi."

"I was wrong, terribly wrong about this approach, it's not feasible." She looked visibly upset, "I'm so sorry."

"What's wrong?" They were confused.

"Everything is freezing, everything is stopping. The new prophecy is not feasible, Ferd must kill the Queen. You had to kill the Caretaker, Houston, you had to!" She wailed with teary eyes. "Kill me! I'll be in place of the Caretaker! Do it before it's too late, please! For the good of all of us!"

"Woah no no no!" They recoiled.

"The Queen will end everything, Houston!" She pressured them, "She's grown as nothing happened, she's stable, she's free! Once the final storm clears she will come to bring the end! Houston, please! Please! Please!" She forcefully exposed her core.

"No!" They pushed her away, "I'm not going to do that!"

"I'm scared! She's terrifying!" Masy cried, "I don't want everyone to suffer because of our selfish desire!"

"Come here..." Houston reached their arms to her. Masy immediately tackled them with her head, wrapped in their arms.

"What's going on..?" Ally opened the door to the room with tired eyes, looking quite shocked after yawning seeing Masy sob into Houston's shoulder.

"I don't know." Houston honestly replied, comforting Masy, "Something's wrong."

Ally settled down on their bed close to Masy, Houston told her all that happened so far.

"The Queen was completely furious, she wants Ferd." Masy was terrified to her heart, "What do we do?"

Ally and Houston both looked at each other, without any words to express their feelings about this sudden situation. They were clearly worried in their eyes.

"I'll confront the Queen." Houston boldly stated, "I'll-"

"We." Ally corrected them, "Together, I'm not letting you get close to her alone."

"But that's-" Masy stammered, "That's suicide! What will you tell her?! What will you do?!"

"The worlds will end when she reunites with Ferd, we cannot let her get to him. I get it now." Houston thought about it, "Every single time, each confrontation, our perception shattered and we ended up in the underworld. There is no way the original prophecy can go on, the King is out."

"We can't let Ferd see Ena either!" Ally added, "He's been getting over her! He's been accepting she's gone for a good while, if them reuniting is truly the cause of the end then..."

"...Either we take down the Queen or the King." Houston came to a conclusion, "Ally! Do you remember anything else from that chess thing you told me about?"

"Chess?" Masy's ears perked up.

"Uh..." Ally thought for a second, "It's really blurry... No, sorry."

"Hazy?" Masy asked.

"Who?" Ally and Houston directed their attention towards Masy.

"She was the one who told me about the new prophecy, how it cannot work, how there is only one way the bubble can warm away the snow. She enjoys playing chess with souls she deems interesting... She's the grandmaster." Masy seemed more calmer, "She enjoys toying with the fate of interesting souls. She even dared to taunt the Queen multiple times, she was the one who even brought her into the underworld! How do both of you know her?"

"My partner, the guardian, she met her once." Ally told her, "I believe Houston's partner also met her one time."

"*Are you sure?*" Houston tilted their head, Ally quietly nodded.

"Both of them are within you, their roles yours to fulfill. I understand it. Houston, Ally..." Her eyes teared up again, "...She is on her way now. If you really wish to confront her, I wish you lots of love."

Houston looked at Ally with a determined expression.

With a tight last hug from Masy just in case they would never return, they quietly left the walker.

The two knights ventured into the deep, dark relentless storm. Treading far from the walker through the thick snow, frozen lakes, and dead nature. Holding each other, being there for each other.

"Oh, Emma, what a wild card you are."

"Your story will be something even grander than I thought."

"Remember this match dear, you will breeze through a tale no one ever has gone through before."

"This match was truly something else... Your decisions may affect even the upcoming prophecy, Lars."

"What do you mean? What the hell are you talking about?"

"What does it truly mean? What will happen in my world?"

"By law, I cannot say. But the most I can tell you..."

"By law, I cannot say. But the most I can tell you..."

"...take great care of Ally, sweetheart."

"...give Houston lots of hugs."

Ally and Houston reach the eye of the storm, calm, silence, just the background wind... and the Queen, descending down at the center in her perfect form. They both looked with a sure, fearless expression. The Queen raised a hand in the sky, utilizing a complete black gauntlet engraved with white glowing runes to summon a grand trident.

Me.
Ferd.
