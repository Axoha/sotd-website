Two fragmented souls came to be in a blank space, as the background moved in the form of the whole universe. Lars' body struggled to move, or walk, but despite the difficulty he managed to get on his two. Houston was sat somewhere far, arms wrapped around their bent legs and the head buried onto their knees.

"Houston..." Lars managed to usher his words, but Houston remained unresponsive.

He walked up to them and sat down with a decently large space in-between them.

"...I brought Sweetie along."

Houston's ears perked up, Lars let go of the cat from their arms. Sweetie ran up to them and started rubbing her head on them. Finally, Houston communicated by simple getting ahold of the cat and wrapping their hands around her. Lars internally decided to not talk first, giving them the space they needed. He looked at the shifting stars, systems, nebulas, like as if everything was clear in their eyes.

"I'm sorry." Houston mumbled.

Lars turned his head towards them, "For what?"

"I killed myself." They sounded exhausted.

"Y'know... There was nothing we could do there, anyway." Lars looked down from the beautiful view, "You've always been my top priority, no matter what."

"We're dead." They continued to mumble.

"It was coming, felt like it was about time. Luck shouldn't have lasted that long really." His eyes turned back at the galaxies.

"I don't understand, why are you here." Houston turned their head to Lars.

"I died with you." He smiled, "I could never imagine a world without you, Houst."

Houston's expression felt touched, but they turned back to their knees, "How are you so positive all the time? I never understood that."

"I don't see the point in focusing on the negatives, ya know?" He looked upwards at the shooting stars, "There's always something to look forward to, something to enjoy in life, yet, at the same time I think it's important to not ignore the negatives."

Houston still looked at the floor, without saying a word.

"You were repressing something, haven't you?" He tilted his head with concern.

"I can never forgive myself." Their own grip on themselves tightened, "I killed my own parents, I stole from people, and despite everything I managed to live a lucky happy life."

"Hm..." Lars thought about what to say, "The way you feel... You're sorry, but not to be excused, it's genuine. It was an accident after all, right?"

"I don't even know..." Their fingers shook.

"Whether to forgive yourself or not, that's for you to decide. Would you rather get eaten by yourself or not, same thing."

Sweetie meowed at Houston.

"What you did has no effect, never has had any effect on the future that developed. Time, in its emotionless nature, continues to move on. What to do with the past... You either let go or sink into it. I believe that no matter what, I'll stand up on my two legs and brace for what comes, no matter the difficulty, no matter the struggle, because I know if I live I'll get to spend a time in bliss again."

He stood up, to embrace the stars.

"There is no definitive good and bad. In every shitty moment is something to enjoy! In every beautiful time anything can occur to ruin it all! Even the best people, or the worst, have something within them that is the complete opposite of what they seem to be. I don't believe in black and white, the universe has colors for a reason!"

Lars reached their hand out to Houston.

"Please take my hand, let's walk through the universe together!"

Houston, with opened eyes, accepted his request. With trembling legs they managed to stand, with Lars supporting them with each step they were about to take.

"Y'know, Houston, we're not dead yet. Let's make the most of it."

At their walk through the galaxies, of course with Sweetie following alongside them, Houston slightly overcame themselves and spoke up with a weak voice. They told Lars about their Caretaker encounter, all about it. The colors of the universe shift colors around them at random. They held hands, tightly.

"...Huh." Lars looked down in thought, "Sounds about right for a typical demon."

"How so..?" Houston felt cold.

"They were manipulating you. Why else would they just focus on your worst experiences and not even mention the wonderful times we've had together after? Scared to give you hope? I bet."

"I guess." They shrug.

"I've noticed something about this place..." He looked up at a bubble, "Like, you see that too? There's so many of these weird peculiar spheres out here, I think they repeat."

"What is it?" Houston was confused.

"Touch one, feels oozy."

Houston's hand drift free though the bubble. Suddenly, their eyes were enlightened with an experience.

"What did that one have?" He spoke eagerly waiting for an answer.

"Oh... Lot to take in." Houston held their head, "I saw a girl, snow leopard, playing videogames on a vintage console with a red fox."

"That one is pretty cool, the girl reminds me of you!" He smiled, "I imagine that's a beloved memory of someone from a different New Babylon city. Let's look through more!"

And Lars ran off, tugging Houston along their way to chase after more bubbles. They reached their fingers out to pop the bubbles, envisioning the memories of others it had stored.

"This one is rad, touch it!" Lars nudged at Houston.

Houston put their palm on the bubble. "The memory of... Oh."

"Pretty cool, right?"

"Yeah! I love that part when..."

"Especially the finale!"

Houston grabbed their cat and made her paw touch the bubble.

"Mreow."

"She agrees!"

Sweetie started purring.

Taking a short break, they decided to lay down on the nothingness to stargaze.
