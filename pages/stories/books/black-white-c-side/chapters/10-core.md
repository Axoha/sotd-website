One step right after another, even if it'll take forever, we'll tread our lives till the very end.
We're going to find an exit out of this place, no matter how beautiful this world is!

Lars grabbed Houston's hand, and they both ran past the crowds of colorful bubbles. An office door appeared at the front of them both. Lars eagerly opened it and went inside. Hallways, a gray carpet floor and a tight hallway with walls covered with unlocked doors and dear buzzing white lights at the ceiling. What an unusual lifeless environment in such cosmos, the two of them with the cat in their hands decided to venture into the interiors of the building anyway. The elevators were not working, the stairs seemed like the only way up. Each floor was the same, and surprisingly, the fridges of some rooms were stacked with fresh food and beverage. It didn't make sense, especially in a dull place like this, but it sure was convenient. In this world they didn't feel thirst or hunger, yet they took a short break to eat food.

"Hey." Lars stared at the ingredients in the fridge, "Let's make a sandwich, like the best one ever."

Houston's eye slowly turned to Lars, who was currently feasting on the cheddar cheese, "...Sure?"

"The ones you see in commercials, come on!" He started taking out all the possible ingredients, "Toasted, cheesy, full of greens and sauce... I'll make one for the both of us!"

During the next five minutes Lars spent all his energy and effort to create the most sandwich ever.

"Ta-Dah." Lars revealed the sandwich on a paper plate with a grin, "Call this the Lars special!"

It was... The most sandwich ever. Houston munched on a whole ball of lettuce with observant eyes. The sandwich didn't look appetizing, and even Lars didn't want to admit that it tasted terribly. It wasn't even toasted. They chuckled after the first bite.

"Come on admit it's pretty okay!" He tried to evade the emotional damage of failure, feeding Sweetie chunks of the ham from the sandwich.

"I'll give it a try." They spoke in a softer tone, getting to the cabinet to look at all the ingredients. There was everything, though, Houston barely made food before.

And so, with swift hands they reached out to take the slices of bread to insert into a toaster. As the bread was in the process of getting toasted, they took a lot of vegetables to slice and cut. Tomatoes, lettuce, pepper. The toaster clanked with a bell and the breads popped out, steaming hot. Houston made the mistake of immediately grabbing them with their bare fingers and they burned themselves. They decided to grab a butter knife and a fork to take out the bread, but Lars immediately stopped them. Ignoring that, they safely and finally managed to get the slices of bread out with the help of Lars, and so they inserted two more slices for themself. With a butter knife Houston cut off a piece of butter to spread over the toasted slice of bread with a satisfying move, they cut some salami and symmetrically placed each wheel on each corner and the center. They got some slices of cheese to place over the salami, then to top off with the lettuce and tomatoes, they made another sandwich which had peppers instead of tomatoes. They topped it off with mayonnaise, and finally placed the second slice on top of the whole culmination of toast. The sauce dripped from the edges, it looked really tasty. Houston took a bite, and they loved it, immediately offering Lars a bite too.

"What a show off." He chuckled, it tasted incredible, "You should make sandwiches more often!"

"I should, huh." They nodded as they went in for another bite, "I'd love to make food."

"I got a buncha books at home, there are some fun recipes you could try." Suddenly, Lars got an idea, "How about in our next missions we experiment, like we take down another massive creature and we take a chunk of it to cook with! Could come up with all kinds of meals, maybe share with the world too..."

"I'd rather not die." They spoke with a full mouth, "Sounds tempting though!"

They spent the next ten minutes eating the toasts. They both decided to leave Lars' sandwich in the fridge with a note, saying that it's for whoever that comes across and that it hopefully won't be all moldy upon its discovery. After they walked out of the kitchen, Houston realized with a facepalm that it would've been a much better idea to throw it in the freezer, but they were too far away from the room by this point. They continued going up the stairs, sometimes taking ladders to the floors above, the buzzing lights were slightly making them feel dizzy. Often times Sweetie would run through the hallways with the full intent of playing, and Houston and Lars would have to chase her and catch her. One time when they were blindly chasing Sweetie, they took a turn and bumped into the back of a... Person.

"Who..." They swiftly turned around, "Dares to disturb my..."

Houston and Lars were on the ground as Sweetie ran back to hide behind them both. The person was incredibly tall and aged with an intimidating look. Gray stricken hair with a grand thick ponytail.

"Souls?" They muttered.

"Sorry!" Lars immediately spurted words which came into his mind, "We didn't mean to-"

"Like kids, playing around in empty spaces." They reached their hands out to both of them, "Get up."

"...Who are you?" Houston was confused, they had to look up to make direct eye contact.

"I am Emilia Jones. This is my home. Who are YOU both???" Her intimidating presence was immediately broken by a more energetic confused expression.

"I'm Lars, this is Houston, I think we're dead. We are trying to get out." Lars talked as he pointed at himself and Houston.

"I need to get him here for this. Follow me." She guided them both through the hallways. "How long have you been here?"

"Like two hours, my best guess, clocks don't work." Lars shrugged.

"So you feel that you haven't been here for long? Got it, there's still hope for you both." She mumbled. "Have you not noticed the floors repeat?"

"They repeat?"

"Have you not looked at the signs?"

Houston and Lars looked at each other, "Nope."

"Damn kids."

Following a specific set of turns and ascensions, Emilia makes way to the rooftop of the invisible building. The cosmic background was finally back, multiple tents were set up on the dark gravel floor of the rooftop with a campfire. Another figure was leaning on the railings at the edge, observing space as usual.

"Akimi, look at this." Emilia stepped aside to reveal Houston and Lars for his eye.

"Souls?" He raised his head.

"Souls! In our home!" She expressed with pure energy again.

The man with long red hair named Akimi swiftly walked up to the duo and the cat. "Nice to meet you both, I'm..." He went in for a handshake, but it was interrupted by Emilia's slap to the wrist.

"They want to leave." She got to the point right away, "Haven't been here long enough for their bodies to decompose in the real world, at least."

"Oh dear." Akimi scratched his head, "Don't mind Emilia, she is slightly cranky."

Houston looked at them both with squinted eyes, "You seem familiar."

"Yeah, we saw both of you as kids in one of the bubbles out there!" Lars added.

"How did you even get here?!" Emilia was still not over their presence.

"We got into this psychological attack by the caretaker, probably died along the way." Lars briefly explained the whole situation.

"Caretaker... That's a nasty one." Akimi took a moment to think.

"You're dead." Emilia told them right away, "If you even get out of this place through that shining glowing sphere which is the 'core', your corpse will be reanimated for a brief moment and you'll eventually just fall back into this blank state. Usually by bleeding out."

"That's what we need!" Lars stepped up, "In the short moment we'll be alive again and we could call the team and get our bodies taken and patched up!" Lars got slightly excited, "Perfect!"

"Not so fast." Emilia halted Lars, "It's guarded, your self may be dragged into lowers levels of this place if you fail. Many have tried."

"Lucky for you two to bump into us!" Akimi proudly exclaimed, "We're geared up to hell and back..."

"Have you tried escaping?" Houston suddenly asked, "Sorry, I couldn't resist to ask..."

Emilia's eye turned to them, "There's no point for us, our bodies don't even exist anymore. Besides, we've lived through a lot anyway." She sat down.

Her line caught Houston's curiosity, they sat down across her with noticeable space in-between, "Um, could you tell me more?"

It was a long while since Emilia talked to someone other than Akimi. With a lot to talk about, she sighed with a smile and started from the beginning of her whole story. Akimi on the other hand led Lars to an old armory within the building, packed with various weaponry he could not comprehend. Light matter weaponry, engineered specifically to erase the dark, manufactured in a world which didn't exist anymore. The Holy Earth. As time went on, both Emilia and Akimi told their own stories to Houston and Lars separately. Emilia got more enthusiastic as she went on, and suddenly her hands flickered with power to further escalate her dramatic presentation. All of a sudden, black ooze came from her fingertips which formed floating silhouettes of the story she was telling near the campfire. Houston and Sweetie were intrigued, listening closely with a slight smile on their face. Akimi told Lars his own tale with lesser drama, but even then, as he walked with Lars to the rooftops his hands shined and created white forms resembling clouds to visualize his story.

"And then..."
"And then..."

Akimi and Emilia both coincidentally said the same thing, as Akimi and Lars finally came back to the rooftop with loads of gear.

"I met her, again."
"I met him, again."

They were both surprised.

"I thought your Houston was super similar to Emilia."
"I thought Lars looked like Akimi at first."

They said the same thing, again. They just looked at each other with a pause.

"The whole eyepatch thing, even if Houston apparently has it for medical reasons. It's the same thing!"
"The messy hair and jolly personality like c'mon it's literally like him."

And they kept adding onto it. Lars and Houston quietly chuckled. They regrouped, all the weapons were put on the floor from the bag.

"Sheesh, haven't seen these in forever." Emilia's eye widened.

"It's slightly overkill, though ammunition is limited. The source for it doesn't exist anymore so keep that in mind." Akimi advised the duo. "At most you'll be able to fire most of these only once."

Houston got ahold of what seemed to be a hyper-advanced assault rifle from the era they just listened to. It was dusty, they blew from their mouth to get most of it off. "I'm not too crazy with weapons."

"Me neither." Lars' continued shuffling through them, "But if it gets us past the guard..."

"We were raised on stuff like this." Akimi talked, "Especially Emilia, give her anything from these and she'd go completely manic on the battlefields!" He made gestures with finger guns, imitating fire.

"I still have all the medals." She chuckled, "Not too proud of 'em, really."

"You'd think with everything we had done during the golden days we'd be smitten down by god themself!" He joked with Emilia.

"Will you help us out?" Houston politely asked with a big eye.

"...No." He turned them down with a frown, "We're old, it has probably been dozens of years since we even ventured out there."

"C'mon you! It's easy!" Emilia encouraged the duo, "Slash some tentacles, get past that dastardly Caretaker, call your buddies in the real world and wait for resurrection of some kind!" Just like Akimi, she imitated the actions with her hands.

"Yeah Houst!" Lars smiled, "We got this."

They couldn't help, but smile too.

"Y'all should probably get going now." Emilia looked at the core of this dimension, "The sooner, the better." She couldn't help but pet Sweetie before they'd depart.

Houston turned to the core of the world. And with Sweetie on their shoulder and Lars by their side, they would depart with a goodbye and good luck.

And as they walked, ever so closer to the core.

Words from a silhouette, not long ago rang in Lars' head.

*"You're one interesting character, Lars."*
*"One interesting guest..."*

*"This chess board between us will define your fate, the outcome of this one round will read your future. The black side, the unforgiving reality, and the white side, the culmination of all your choices you make."*

*"But the most I can tell you..."*
*"...give Houston lots of hugs."*

The floor beneath them turned into a chess board, they both readied their weapons immediately. The core was just within reach, but an impossible jump would be required to go through the sphere. The darkness around them both manifested into the head of the Caretaker...

It seemed exactly like the chess match Lars has played before.

Houston was shook by its mere presence, but Lars nudged closer to them and offered a hand. They gripped it, tightly, as they both stood against the impossible. The core was shrouded in the Caretaker's darkness, no soul may escape death.

They held hands together.

The Caretaker opened up with devastating moves, summoning black limbs from the ground which were easy to evade. It was clear that the both of them would have to use it's body to even reach the core somehow, and even then they had to plow through the darkness it was shrouded in. In the worst case Sweetie would be the one to escape and call the team. Both of them saved the ammunition of the weaponry, deciding to use it only when needed. The Caretaker continued with its relentless strikes, repeating the same moves, exhausting its victims. Again, again, again. For what it seemed to last like eternity, Houston finally opened fire to get rid of the hands and make space, but erasing one only made them duplicate. They'd decide to make a run for it, shooting at what came in the way, but more and more limbs appeared to drag them onto the floor. The hands had a firm grip on every inch of their body. The hands were relentless, unforgiving, and sharp. It seemed over, until a black sharp javelin was flung directly into one of the Caretaker's many right eyes, shattering it completely like glass.

"I can't just sit back there and watch such an amusing duo suffer!"

A familiar voice, in an unfamiliar form. Emilia's voice rung from a beastly body, composed of black vine and brute force. Mocking the Caretaker for her own pride, another beastly body appeared, formed by a white glow, freeing the duo and the cat from the Caretaker's grasp.

"Knees hurt..." Akimi's otherworldly voice sound from the beastly angelic form as he helped the duo get up on its legs again.

"SHUT IT!!!" A deafeningly loud yell from Emilia's devilish form came, "You *NEED* exercise, I HAVE BEEN TELLING YOU."

"STOP BEING SO AGGRESSIVE ABOUT IT!!!" They argued like an old, married couple.

Both Emilia and Akimi helped the duo fend off the limbs, no matter how many times they duplicated. They urged them on, cheering them on, Houston was ahead of Lars until their legs completely collapsed out of exhaustion.

"Go..." They grumbled, "Get to the core..!"

"But... You!?" Lars wasn't willing to just leave them behind.

"You'll return... here anyways..." They looked at him with a smile.

Sweetie leapt off Lars' shoulder to stay with Houston.

Bringing a tear to his eye, he decided to do what they had to, smiling despite the difficulty. With the last ammo left, he managed to break through the core's darkness and with slight hesitation he dived into the light.

Blinding, white, light.

Sun, light.

Grass, smoke, trees, birds, flowers.

Bugs, animals, wreckage, life.

Blue, sky, clouds, sunlight.

Lars treaded the wild grass with an injured body that shouldn't be able to walk, but through sheer determination...

Houston, Sweetie, the injuries were fatal, gory, they laid in the wreckage, dead, but despite everything...

"We did it." Lars looked at the sky with a smile, "We escaped."

He held an device that would send their current location to the rest of Omega and the New Babylon's emergency teams. With a click, their location was sent. They would be found, they would live on.

"We did it..." With a hiccup, he started crying with a smile. He treaded back to the wreckage and laid down next to Houston's dead body. Wrapping his arms around them, they decided to die while giving them hugs.

"Let's become racing champions... Let's live on, forever."
