After our father left without a word, it was dark. We were left to wander around aimlessly in the ruins of the once beautiful crystal palace of quartz. There was no one to drive us forward anymore, there was no one to take care of us. As one of the first of his children, I picked up on a leading role right when the darkness grew deeper.

Angels. They arrived without a welcome, or say. They arrived for one reason, to strip us away from our freedom. The light, their holy roars, it is an image I wish to forget. They sent not one, but hundreds of colossal Guardian Angels to decimate our home and leave us with nothing. We were not born to fight, yet we fought back valiantly.

They all died, the first children died, the newborn died. Once there was nothing left, they banished us into darkness. Those who perished in the light remain in my memory, the brave souls which shall not be forgotten, ever. I survived only by a miracle, but a burning scar through my whole chest still lingers, one that I cannot heal from ever.

In the pitch-black darkness we adapted, and after millions of years, one day we were set free.
